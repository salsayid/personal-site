Launch Day: Watching Europa Clipper Take Off

October 8, 2025

I woke up at 5 AM, adrenaline preceding the sunrise. It was time — Europa Clipper would lift off today. Years of design, simulation, and testing culminating in a few minutes of controlled violence.

The moment of ignition is always surreal. A silent flower of fire, then a low rumble that shakes through your screen and into your bones. It's a testament to human ingenuity - our ability to direct such immense power with this precision.

As I watched the telemetry data stream in, each successful milestone confirmation felt like a collective exhale. This was more than just another launch — it was humanity's first dedicated mission to study Jupiter's ocean moon. We're about to explore one of the solar system's most promising locations for extraterrestrial life.
